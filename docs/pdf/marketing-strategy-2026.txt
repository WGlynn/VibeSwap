1. Positioning

1.1 The Problem

Every major DEX today has the same fundamental flaw: the order in which trades
execute determines who profits. Miners, validators, and MEV bots extract value
by front-running, sandwiching, and reordering transactions. Users subsidize this
extraction with every swap.

The numbers are staggering:

- Over $1.4 billion extracted via MEV on Ethereum alone (2020-2025)
- Average retail trader loses 0.5-2% per trade to sandwich attacks
- Front-running bots consistently outbid real users for execution priority

This is not a feature of decentralization. It is a failure of mechanism design.

1.2 Our Solution

VibeSwap eliminates MEV through 10-second batch auctions:

1.  Commit Phase (8s): Users submit encrypted order hashes with deposits
2.  Reveal Phase (2s): Orders are revealed with optional priority bids
3.  Settlement: Orders are shuffled using cryptographic randomness (Fisher-Yates
    with XORed secrets) and executed at a single uniform clearing price

No one can front-run because orders are encrypted during submission. No one can
sandwich because all trades in a batch execute at the same price. Priority bids
pay for urgency without affecting the clearing price for others.

One-liner: Trade without getting robbed. Build without getting diluted.

2. Target Audiences

2.1 Primary: Degens Who Hate Front-Running

Who they are: Active on-chain traders (10+ trades/week) who have been
sandwiched, front-run, or otherwise MEV-extracted. They’re crypto-native,
technically literate, and vocally frustrated with current DEX infrastructure.

Where they are: Crypto Twitter, Discord trading servers, DeFi Llama
leaderboards, on-chain analytics dashboards.

What they want: A DEX that doesn’t steal from them. They will trade more volume
on a fair platform because they keep more of their returns.

Message: “Every trade on Uniswap, someone is front-running you. On VibeSwap,
trades are encrypted and batch-settled. Same price for everyone. Zero MEV.”

2.2 Secondary: Builders Who Want Fair Launches

Who they are: Protocol founders, token creators, and project teams who want to
launch tokens without VC extraction, insider trading, or sniping bots.

Where they are: GitHub, Ethereum research forums, hackathons, builder
communities (Buildspace, ETHGlobal alumni).

What they want: A launch platform where early buyers don’t get exploited by
bots, and the team doesn’t have to give away 20% to VCs for “distribution.”

Message: “Launch on VibeSwap. No sniping bots. No front-running. Your community
gets the same price as everyone else. Fair launch means fair launch.”

2.3 Tertiary: Communities Tired of Extractive Platforms

Who they are: DAO members, governance participants, and crypto idealists who
believe DeFi should serve users, not rent-seekers.

Where they are: Governance forums, DAO tooling communities, crypto podcasts
focused on governance and mechanism design.

What they want: Proof that Cooperative Capitalism works. A protocol that
distributes value to contributors, not extractors.

Message: “VibeSwap’s fees go to liquidity providers and governance stakers via
Shapley distribution — mathematically proportional to marginal contribution. No
pre-mine. No VC cut. The protocol rewards the people who build it.”
