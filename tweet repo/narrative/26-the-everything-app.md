Everyone says they're building the everything app.

We actually might be. Here's the difference.

Most "everything apps" are one product that bolts on features until it collapses under its own weight. Chat app adds payments. Payments app adds social. Social app adds marketplace. None of it coheres.

We started from mechanism design. The core isn't a product — it's a coordination layer. Batch auctions. Commit-reveal. Shapley value distribution. Game-theoretic incentive alignment.

From that foundation:

DEX. Governance. Identity. Lending. Insurance. Cross-chain messaging. AI agents. DePIN. RWA tokenization.

Not bolted on. Derived from the same primitives.

When your foundation is "fair coordination between adversarial parties," everything else is just a use case.

The everything app isn't built by adding everything. It's built by getting the foundation right and letting everything emerge.
