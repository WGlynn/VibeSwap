> mechanism :: rubric

Every post fills four slots.
Blank slots reject the submission.

[ REQUIRED FIELDS ]
[Claim] // the specific position being advanced
[Evidence] // what supports the claim
[Acknowledge] // what prior context already said
[Push] // what you're advancing or disputing
Blank required slot → post rejected.
Your turn to either think or stay out.
[ WHAT THIS KILLS ]
  • Drive-by reactions with no argument
  • Restating the thread's consensus as if it were a counter
  • Claiming novelty without saying what's novel
  • Throwing evidence-free takes into technical threads
  • Positional-dominance posturing (Keri-pattern)
The rubric IS the moderation. Math, not manual.

> mechanism :: scoring

Reputation compounds on model-advancement.
Not on reach.

01
Shapley-weighted reputation
Contribution to thread quality, not upvotes. Fair share under cooperative-game theory.
→ signal rewarded
02
Novelty multiplier
Restating thread consensus scores zero. Every post has to add something.
→ no echo chamber
03
Attestation-challenge on evidence
Any reader can tag [Evidence] as challenged. 24h dispute window. Resolution reputation-weighted.
→ grounded claims
04
Append-only log
Posts don't edit. Addenda are new replies. The record is cryptographically permanent.
→ no revisionism
05
Prescriptive-before-retrospective
Rubric enforces shape at submission. Scoring applies after. You pass the gate, then you get judged.
→ structural filter
