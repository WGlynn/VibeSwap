▸ Story Mode

Gamified vibe coding.

The original choose-your-own-adventure game loop — running on an LLM.

You remember the old text-adventure games: the screen describes the world, gives you a list of moves, you pick one, the story branches. Story Mode is that exact loop pointed at real work. The agent does a turn, then hands you a ranked menu of what to do next. You reply with a number. The dungeon master is a mind now, and the dungeon is your actual project.

One reply, 7,1,2, queues three moves in order. That multi-pick is the whole trick — you are not clicking through a wizard, you are directing.

Why it is more than a menu

The original game AI

Branch trees were the first thing ever called game AI. Story Mode is that loop, but the branches are generated live and the choices do real work instead of advancing a plot.

A non-zero-sum feed

It is a recommendation feed, like YouTube's — but inverted. The algorithm optimizes for the move you actually want next, not for keeping you hooked. Same machinery, opposite incentive.

You are the protagonist

The menu is built from how you decide, so every session is your own story. You stay the author of the path; the agent just renders each branch the moment you choose it.

How it works

  1. Every turn ends in a fork. The agent appends a menu of the ten most likely next moves, ranked, each short enough to run by number.
  2. You steer with a keystroke. Reply 3 to run one. Reply 5,4,1 to run a sequence in order. No re-typing, no composing a prompt.
  3. The menu learns your hand. It tracks how often your real move was one of the ten (its catch-rate) and reweights toward your patterns. It gets better at being you.
The quiet part: everyone who runs it becomes the main character of their own story — and all of those stories arc toward the same place. Maximum agency, one shared destination. You choose your own way there.
