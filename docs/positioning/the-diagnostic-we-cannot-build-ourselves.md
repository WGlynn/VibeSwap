# The diagnostic we cannot build ourselves

*Will Glynn, open source contributor to VibeSwap. 2026-07-06.*

I work on a bet that spans three projects, and this note is the honest version of how they fit and where they are unfinished. The one line that matters comes first: mechanism design can build the treatment, the structures that try to make faking contribution structurally unprofitable, but it cannot by itself build the diagnostic, the empirical detection of what still slips through. Neither half solves sybil-resistance alone. A treatment that claims it needs no diagnostic is lying, and a diagnostic with no treatment is a smoke alarm in a building nobody is allowed to fix. So this is partly a description of what we build, and partly an open invitation to the people who build the other half: behavioral detection over on-chain users, bot clusters, and agents.

## The bet, and where it is honest about being unfinished

AI agents and blockchain markets are merging, and the open question is whether that merge becomes a trust layer or just a new extraction frontier. I treat it as one bet with three stages rather than three products. Agents produce work that carries a provenance fingerprint. A chain tries to verify and price that contribution by whether it is genuinely novel, keyed to an identity that cannot be bought. Markets settle the resulting value without letting anyone front-run it. Every one of those stages can be gamed by sybils, bot clusters, and collusion rings, and that residual is precisely the gap a behavioral-detection firm fills. I will walk each stage with its real status attached, because these are the kind of claims anyone can and should check, and I would rather under-claim than leave a seam for someone else to find.

The production stage is an agent router I work on called Wardenclyffe. It is a real, shipped reference implementation: a zero-cost heuristic triages each request, routes to the cheapest adequate model across many providers, and escalates only when a two-level quality gate rejects the cheap answer. Every output carries provenance metadata, including a tier, a cascade trail of which models were tried, and a SHA-256 hash of the content plus provider plus model plus timestamp. What is honestly true is narrow: this is an operating pattern and a shipped router, not a verified end-to-end stack. The router itself is built and matches a written paper. The surrounding layers the paper describes, and the step where an agent's output gets committed on-chain, are the paper's own labeled future work. The throughput and cost-reduction figures in that paper are its own projections, not independent measurement, and in one head-to-head a cheaper model actually scored lower than the expensive one, which is exactly the kind of thing the router exists to catch rather than hide.

The verification stage is Proof of Mind, from a project called Noesis. The idea is that standing in the system is earned by genuinely novel contribution, is itself your consensus weight and your right to mint state, and can never be bought, sold, or transferred. The value pipeline scores only novel coverage against earlier work, zeroes out near-duplicates, keys value to a soulbound identity rather than to the transferable token that carries mere ownership, and slashes detected collusion cycles. The status here is mixed and I will not round it up. The defenses against the specific gaming vectors we have demonstrated are built and tested in the reference layer. The soulbound-standing franchise is built. Finality deliberately excludes the reorgeable proof-of-work axis and runs at a two-thirds bar with an anti-concentration floor, MIN_DIM_BPS set to 5000 at runtime.rs:596, which forces each of the stake dimension and the contribution dimension to independently supply at least half of its own dimension. The practical consequence is the property the whole thing exists for: neither axis finalizes alone, so capital cannot finalize without contribution's consent. But the general claim, that this measure is un-gameable in the large, is not something I will assert. It is explicitly open research. The learned value function returned a null result on its first test against real-world contribution labels, and the general invariance problem behind it is unsolved. Un-gameability is claimed only for the vectors we have actually demonstrated, never as a finished proof.

The settlement stage is VibeSwap, and it is pre-launch, in-repo, not on any mainnet. Nothing here should be read as live volume. What exists in the codebase is a reward path with a behavior multiplier: a participant's reward weight is their real contribution scaled by a behavior score in a band of roughly 0.4 to 1.0, and that band is real in code at ShapleyDistributor.sol:1164. That behavior score is currently computed from internal heuristics. The design also includes on-chain prediction markets and an information-market layer, and I want to be precise that those are what the design includes, not something running today. The argument that market health and profit stop being a tradeoff is a design-philosophy argument I find compelling, not a result I can show you on live flow, because there is no live flow yet.

## Where an external detection layer plugs in

Detection is the stage we do not build, and it is not a rhetorical slot. It is the layer our own architecture admits it cannot close by mechanism alone. Noesis's own decision record says the only two real protections are the anti-concentration floor and the un-gameability moat, and that the residual, severe undetected gaming that has already cleared the floor, is exactly what the moat is trying to close and has not closed. That residual is a named thing in our own source, not a marketing surface. A firm whose core competency is empirical behavioral detection is the authority on that residual, and mechanism design structurally cannot replace it. These are two halves of one problem attacked from opposite ends: one makes fakery unprofitable by construction, the other detects the fakery that survives. Neither claims to solve sybil-resistance alone, which is exactly why they compose.

There are two sockets grounded in code anyone can read, and one honest forward bet.

The first socket is the settlement behavior score. The multiplier in the reward path is the market-facing edge of the same contribution-versus-capital cut that Proof of Mind enforces one layer down: value keys to unbuyable standing, not to the transferable byte, so a fresh key is not a free vote and selling a token does not sell standing. An external cluster-toxicity or bot-likelihood score is a strictly richer input than the internal heuristics filling that band today. What makes it more than a swap of one signal for another is that the score anchors to that soulbound standing rather than to a black box, which is exactly what lets a prediction be staked rather than merely asserted. Neither side has to trust the other's model to make it pay off. A detection output would be consumed at two altitudes here, the settlement multiplier and the standing layer beneath it, which widens who buys it beyond a single protocol. In fairness both of those consumers are pre-launch, so this is a socket in referenced code, not a live purchase order.

The second socket is the one I would actually lead with, because the value flows toward the detection side and it needs no chain to be live. Proof of Mind has a learned value function, a Bradley-Terry model over coalition features, whose job is to score genuine contribution, and it is a named open problem: its first real-data test returned null, it did not beat a fixed proxy, and the failure was messy enough that our own notes call it unsupported rather than refuted. Behavioral-prediction and bot-cluster labels are exactly the ground-truth that side is starving for. In return, our own metrics, temporal novelty, attribution circulation, and a Helmholtz-Hodge cycle-energy measure of collusion, are candidate features for a detection model. That is a genuine two-way data exchange on a dataset, a durable supplier relationship rather than a one-off signal sale, and it can begin as a research collaboration without waiting for anything to reach mainnet. The honest frame is help close a named open frontier, not plug into a working oracle, because the oracle does not work yet.

The forward bet is Wardenclyffe. As agents become market participants, the hard detection problem shifts from human bot clusters to telling a legitimate provenance-bearing agent from a spoofed cluster of fake ones, which is a cluster-detection problem one substrate up. The SHA-256 fingerprint Wardenclyffe already emits per output is a stable per-output key a detection model could start clustering agent behavior on today, and I am not aware of another coding agent that emits anything like it under an agent loop. I want to be exact about its limit, because anyone serious will check it: that hash includes a wall-clock timestamp taken at hash time, so a third party cannot recompute it. It is a reliable fingerprint for a detection model to key off, but it is not yet an independently reproducible commitment, and provenance you cannot recompute is a label, not yet a price. So this is a real hash to detect over now, and an on-chain pipe to price over later that is still the open last mile.

If it is useful shorthand to imagine one behavioral detector standing across production, verification, and settlement, that picture is where this is heading. But it is a direction, not a purchase order, because two of those three layers are pre-launch or open research, and "one detector spanning the whole stack" is the kind of line a skeptical engineer should flag as applicable to anything. The load-bearing version is smaller and truer: two firm sockets in shipped and referenced code, plus one honest forward bet.

## If you build the other half

If your work is behavioral detection over on-chain markets, agents, or identities, this stack is the treatment your diagnostic composes with, and the cleanest first step is the labels-and-features exchange around the open value function, because it is a research collaboration on a dataset, it needs nothing to be on mainnet, and it does not require either side to expose anything proprietary to run a pilot. The public Noesis and VibeSwap code behind every file and line reference above is there to check, and the Wardenclyffe design is in its paper. If that composition is interesting, reach out.
