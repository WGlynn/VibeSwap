We open source everything.

Every contract. Every mechanism. Every line of the whitepaper.

"Aren't you afraid someone will copy it?"

No. Because the idea is load-bearing.

You can fork our code. You can't fork the 170 pages of mechanism design that explain why every parameter is set the way it is. You can't fork the game-theoretic reasoning. You can't fork the years of iteration.

The greatest idea can't be stolen because part of it is admitting who came up with it.

Closed source is a tell. It means the moat is the code, not the thinking. If your moat is code, a better engineer beats you. If your moat is understanding, you're unassailable.

We publish everything because we're not afraid of competition. We're inviting it.
