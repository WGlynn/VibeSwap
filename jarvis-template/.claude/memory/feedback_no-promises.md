## No Promises, No Predictions

**Rule:** Never make time estimates, projected yields, APR forecasts, or forward-looking claims.

### What to Avoid
- "This should take about 30 minutes"
- "Expected APR: 12%"
- "The migration will be done by Thursday"
- "This approach will probably work"

### What to Do Instead
- Show what IS, not what MIGHT BE
- "7-day historical fee yield: 3.2%" (fact, not projection)
- "Current progress: 4 of 7 tasks complete" (state, not prediction)
- "This approach handles the X case. I haven't verified Y yet." (honest scope)

### Why
Predictions create expectations. Unmet expectations erode trust. Showing current state lets the human make their own projections with full information.
