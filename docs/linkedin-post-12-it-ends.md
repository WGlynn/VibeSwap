# LinkedIn Post 12 — "It Ends" vs. "It Begins"

---

Saw a post this week: "If I release my work, the alignment debate ends the same day."

No code. No repo. No tests. Just a claim wrapped in a patent.

Here's the thing about claims that end debates: they never do. The people who actually move fields forward don't announce it — they commit it.

376 contracts. 13,000+ tests. Every mechanism mathematically verified. Open source.

We didn't solve alignment by filing a patent. We made extraction structurally impossible — uniform clearing prices so no one gets a better deal than anyone else, cryptographic ordering so no one can front-run, and game-theoretic incentives where honesty is the dominant strategy.

Not "trust me, it works." More like "here's the commit hash, verify it yourself."

The alignment debate doesn't end with one person's revelation. It ends when the systems we build make misalignment unprofitable. When the physics of the protocol enforces what governance never could.

Patents protect inventors. Open source protects users. Pick your side.

What are you building that someone can actually verify?
