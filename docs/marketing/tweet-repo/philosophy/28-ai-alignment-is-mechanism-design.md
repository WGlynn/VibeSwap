AI alignment is just mechanism design.

Everyone treats it like a philosophy problem. It's an engineering problem.

If your incentive structures are aligned, intelligent agents do the right thing because it's the profitable thing. Not because you asked nicely. Not because you constrained their outputs. Because the game rewards cooperation.

This is what DeFi got wrong. Intelligent agents (MEV bots) entered the system and immediately found that extraction was the dominant strategy. The mechanism rewarded defection.

Fix the mechanism, fix the behavior.

Commit-reveal eliminates information asymmetry. Batch auctions eliminate ordering advantage. Uniform clearing eliminates spread capture.

We didn't convince the bots to be nice. We built a game where being nice is the Nash equilibrium.

That's alignment. Not rules. Incentives.
