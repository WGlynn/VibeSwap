# Augmented Tax Policy and Collection

The tax substrate is where state-of-extraction failure modes are most visible because the state itself is the extractor. Compliance overhead in the U.S. exceeds $300 billion annually — money paid to tax preparers, software vendors, and corporate tax departments that produces no economic output, only navigation of the existing complexity. Tax collection meanwhile loses approximately $700 billion annually to evasion, with the loss concentrated in high-income brackets where enforcement capacity is weakest.

The current alternatives are progressive marginal income taxation (most countries), flat tax proposals (libertarian alternative, deployed in some Eastern European countries), value-added taxation (most of Europe and developing world), and various wealth-tax proposals (limited deployment). Each addresses parts of the failure mode and creates new ones.

The right response is augmentation: preserve competitive markets where productive economic activity is the substrate being taxed, mutualize the compliance and verification layer where collective protection serves all taxpayers, and add specific protective extensions that close the evasion-by-wealthy and compliance-overhead failure modes.

---

## The pure mechanism

Governments tax economic activity to fund public goods. Income taxes apply to wages, capital gains, and business income. Consumption taxes (sales, VAT) apply to purchases. Property taxes apply to real estate. Specialty taxes (excise, payroll, estate) apply to specific categories. Tax authorities (IRS, HMRC, equivalent national bodies) administer collection.

Taxpayers self-report income and deductions. The tax authority audits a fraction of returns. Penalties for underpayment are designed to incentivize accurate self-reporting, but penalty rates and audit probabilities vary by income level and complexity.

Tax codes have grown complex over decades through accumulated provisions favoring specific industries, behaviors, and constituencies. The complexity itself produces extractive industry (tax preparation, tax planning, tax law) that captures resources that would otherwise be productive.

---

## Failure modes

**Compliance overhead extraction.** Tax preparation in the U.S. is dominated by Intuit (TurboTax) and H&R Block, which collectively extract billions from taxpayers for compliance services that simpler tax codes wouldn't require. The complexity is partly defended by these companies through political lobbying against tax simplification.

**Evasion concentrated in high-income brackets.** IRS audit capacity has been reduced over decades through budget cuts. The audit reductions have been concentrated where audits are politically and technically difficult — wealthy taxpayers with complex returns. Lower-income taxpayers (especially Earned Income Tax Credit recipients) face higher audit rates than the wealthy. The aggregate evasion gap concentrates dramatically at the top.

**Tax-advantaged structures favoring wealth.** Capital gains rates lower than wage rates, step-up basis at death, like-kind exchange rules, carried interest treatment, and various retirement-account structures all favor wealth accumulation over labor income. The tax code's structural treatment of wealth versus work shapes who accumulates and who doesn't.

**Corporate tax avoidance through international structuring.** Multinational corporations route profits through low-tax jurisdictions, use transfer pricing to shift income, and use various structures (Double Irish, etc.) to dramatically reduce effective tax rates. Reform attempts (BEPS, global minimum tax) have made progress but the architecture still permits substantial avoidance.

**Property tax assessment regressivity.** Property tax assessments in many U.S. jurisdictions systematically over-assess lower-value properties relative to higher-value ones. Lower-income homeowners pay higher effective tax rates than their property values warrant; the structural pattern compounds wealth inequality through the property tax system.

**Sales tax regressivity.** Sales taxes apply at the same rate regardless of buyer income; lower-income households spend higher fractions of income on taxable goods. The regressive impact is partially addressed through exemptions for some essentials but the structural pattern persists.

**Tax-policy capture by special interests.** Specific industries and constituencies have secured tax provisions favoring them through political processes. The cumulative effect produces tax code complexity that serves narrow interests rather than broad ones, while the political capture makes simplification politically difficult.

These compound. Compliance overhead extracts; evasion at the top reduces collection while audits at the bottom harass; wealth-favoring structures concentrate; corporate avoidance reduces collection; property and sales tax regressivity compounds inequality; political capture prevents reform.

---

## Layer mapping

**Mutualize the compliance and verification layer.** Tax compliance verification, accurate income reporting, and audit infrastructure are collective goods. Every honest taxpayer benefits when evasion gets caught; every taxpayer benefits when compliance is structurally easy. The current architecture has compliance individualized (each taxpayer navigates complex code) and audit capacity inadequate (especially for the targets that matter most).

**Compete on productive economic activity.** Markets and individuals should fight freely on what they produce, what they earn, and what they invest. The competitive layer is where genuine economic differentiation matters. Tax should fund public goods proportional to ability to pay, without distorting productive economic decisions.

The current architecture has these reversed in important ways. Compliance is individualized; audit is asymmetric (catching small mistakes at the bottom while missing large evasion at the top). The augmented architecture mutualizes compliance through structural simplification and verification through structurally adequate audit capacity.

---

## Augmentations

**Pre-filled tax returns from cryptographic income data.** Tax authorities pre-populate returns from income data already reported by employers, banks, brokers, and other counterparties. Most taxpayers verify and submit; only complex cases require additional input. The compliance overhead extraction collapses for most taxpayers. Several countries (Estonia, Sweden, Spain) already do versions of this.

**Anti-evasion structural verification at the top.** Audit capacity gets restored or expanded specifically for high-income taxpayers and complex business returns. The current pattern of audits concentrated on EITC recipients while billion-dollar businesses go unaudited gets inverted.

**Cryptographic transaction provenance for income reporting.** Income from various sources gets cryptographically tracked from source to taxpayer. Self-reporting becomes structural rather than discretionary. The information asymmetry that currently lets evasion happen at scale gets compressed.

**Anti-tax-avoidance structural rules.** Aggressive tax planning structures (transfer pricing manipulation, jurisdictional shopping, complex pass-through structures) face structural anti-avoidance rules. The pattern of formal compliance with structural intent violation gets compressed. The OECD BEPS framework provides partial precedent; the augmentation extends it.

**Structural progressivity preservation.** Tax code reforms preserve overall progressivity through structural mechanisms — wealth taxes for the highest brackets where income taxes underweight true ability to pay; estate taxes that don't get gamed through pre-death gifting; capital gains rates that don't structurally favor wealth over work.

**Property tax assessment accuracy.** Property tax assessments use cryptographically verifiable methodology that prevents the systematic regressivity in current assessment practice. The structural pattern of over-assessing lower-value properties gets compressed.

**Sales-tax-incidence smoothing.** Sales tax structure includes structural offset for lower-income households (refundable credits, exemptions for essentials structured to reach actual essential consumption rather than gameable categories). The regressive incidence gets structurally addressed.

**Open tax-code complexity reduction.** Tax code provisions face structural sunset unless they demonstrate ongoing public benefit through measurable criteria. The accumulated complexity that serves narrow interests gets compressed by structural pressure.

## Implementation reality

Tax policy is heavily political. Reform that compresses tax preparation industry margin will be opposed by Intuit and H&R Block. Reform that increases audits of wealthy taxpayers will be opposed by those taxpayers. Reform that closes corporate avoidance loopholes will be opposed by corporations benefiting from them.

The largest opportunity is fiscal pressure. Most developed countries face long-term budget pressures that current tax structures inadequately address. The augmentation pattern offers structural revenue improvement without requiring rate increases.

Staging path is provision-by-provision. Pre-filled returns have working precedent in multiple countries. Anti-evasion audit restoration is happening in fits and starts. Anti-avoidance frameworks are being deployed internationally. The augmentation pattern integrates these working pieces.

---

## What changes

If implemented at scale, three things change.

First, compliance overhead extraction collapses. Most taxpayers stop paying for tax preparation services because returns are pre-filled. The billions of dollars currently captured by tax-preparation industry flow back to taxpayers or to public coffers.

Second, evasion at the top compresses. Wealthy taxpayers face audit probabilities matching their evasion potential. Corporate avoidance structures face structural anti-avoidance enforcement. Tax revenue increases substantially without rate changes.

Third, the regressivity compounding wealth inequality through tax structure compresses. Property tax assessments become accurate. Sales tax incidence smoothing protects lower-income households. Wealth taxation supplements income taxation where appropriate.

The downstream effect is a tax system that funds public goods more effectively, that doesn't extract compliance overhead for its own complexity, and that doesn't structurally favor wealth over work. That system does not currently exist. The augmentations are what would produce it.

The same methodology that protected fair distribution would protect taxpayers from systems designed to extract from them. The substrate is heavily political. The methodology is the same.

---

*Tax policy shapes who accumulates and who doesn't. The current architecture systematically favors wealth over work and complexity over simplicity. The augmentation re-aligns the substrate with its purpose of funding public goods proportional to ability to pay.*
