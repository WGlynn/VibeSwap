# The diagnostic we cannot build ourselves

*Will Glynn, open source contributor to VibeSwap. 2026-07-06.*

I work on a bet that spans three projects, and this is the honest version of how they fit and where they are unfinished. The line that matters comes first: mechanism design can build the treatment, the structures that make faking contribution structurally unprofitable, but it cannot by itself build the diagnostic, the empirical detection of what still slips through. Neither half solves sybil-resistance alone. A treatment that claims it needs no diagnostic is lying, and a diagnostic with no treatment is a smoke alarm in a building nobody is allowed to fix. That division of labor is the whole point. Everything below is where it becomes concrete, and it doubles as an open invitation to the people who build the other half: behavioral detection over on-chain users, bot clusters, and agents.

## The bet, and where it is unfinished

AI agents and blockchain markets are merging, and the open question is whether that merge becomes a trust layer or just a new extraction frontier. I treat it as one bet with three stages, not three products. Agents produce work that carries a provenance fingerprint. A chain verifies and prices that contribution by whether it is genuinely novel, keyed to an identity that cannot be bought. Markets settle the value without letting anyone front-run it. Every stage can be gamed by sybils, bot clusters, and collusion rings, and that residual is the gap a detection firm fills. I will attach real status to each, because these are claims anyone can check, and I would rather under-claim than leave a seam for someone else to find.

Production is an agent router I work on called Wardenclyffe. It is a shipped reference implementation: a zero-cost heuristic triages each request, routes to the cheapest adequate model, and escalates only when a two-level quality gate rejects the cheap answer. Every output carries provenance metadata: a tier, a cascade trail of which models were tried, and a SHA-256 hash of the content, provider, model, and timestamp. What is honestly true is narrow. The router is built and matches a written paper; the surrounding layers, and the step that commits an agent's output on-chain, are that paper's own future work, and its throughput and cost figures are projections, not independent measurement. In one head-to-head a cheaper model scored lower than the expensive one, which is exactly what the router exists to catch rather than hide.

Verification is Proof of Mind, from a project called Noesis. Standing in the system is earned by genuinely novel contribution, is itself your consensus weight and your right to mint state, and can never be bought, sold, or transferred. The value pipeline scores only novel coverage against earlier work, zeroes out near-duplicates, keys value to a soulbound identity rather than to the transferable token that carries mere ownership, and slashes detected collusion cycles. The status is mixed and I will not round it up. Defenses against the gaming vectors we have demonstrated are built and tested; the soulbound-standing franchise is built; finality excludes the reorgeable proof-of-work axis and runs at a two-thirds bar with an anti-concentration floor (MIN_DIM_BPS 5000, runtime.rs:596) that forces the stake and contribution dimensions to each supply at least half of their own, so neither finalizes alone and capital cannot finalize without contribution's consent. But the general claim, that this is un-gameable in the large, I will not assert: it is open research. The learned value function returned a null result on its first test against real contribution labels, and the invariance problem behind it is unsolved. Un-gameability is claimed only for the vectors we have actually demonstrated.

Settlement is VibeSwap, and it is pre-launch, in-repo, not on any mainnet. Nothing here is live volume. What exists in code is a reward path with a behavior multiplier: reward weight is real contribution scaled by a behavior score in a band of roughly 0.4 to 1.0 (ShapleyDistributor.sol:1164), currently computed from internal heuristics. The design also includes on-chain prediction markets and an information-market layer, and those are what the design includes, not what runs today. That market health and profit stop being a tradeoff is a design argument I find compelling, not a result I can show on live flow, because there is no live flow yet.

## Where an external detection layer plugs in

Detection is the stage we do not build, and it is not a rhetorical slot. Our own architecture admits it cannot close by mechanism alone: Noesis's decision record says the only two real protections are the anti-concentration floor and the un-gameability moat, and that the severe undetected gaming which has already cleared the floor is exactly what the moat has not closed. That residual is named in our own source. A firm whose competency is empirical behavioral detection is the authority on it, and mechanism design cannot replace it. Two halves of one problem: one makes fakery unprofitable by construction, the other detects the fakery that survives.

Two sockets are grounded in code anyone can read, plus one honest forward bet.

The first is the settlement behavior score. That multiplier is the market-facing edge of the same contribution-versus-capital cut Proof of Mind enforces one layer down: value keys to unbuyable standing, so a fresh key is not a free vote and selling a token does not sell standing. An external cluster-toxicity or bot-likelihood score is a strictly richer input than today's internal heuristics, and because it anchors to soulbound standing rather than a black box, a prediction can be staked rather than merely asserted, so neither side has to trust the other's model. Honest caveat: both consumers are pre-launch, so this is a socket in referenced code, not a live purchase order.

The second is the one I would lead with, because the value flows toward the detection side and it needs no chain to be live. Proof of Mind's learned value function, a Bradley-Terry model over coalition features meant to score genuine contribution, is a named open problem: its first real-data test returned null and did not beat a fixed proxy. Behavioral-prediction and bot-cluster labels are exactly the ground truth it is starving for, and in return our metrics (temporal novelty, attribution circulation, and a Helmholtz-Hodge cycle-energy measure of collusion) are candidate features for a detection model. That is a two-way data exchange on a dataset, a durable relationship rather than a one-off signal sale, and it can begin as research without waiting for mainnet. The honest frame is help close a named open frontier, not plug into a working oracle, because the oracle does not work yet.

The forward bet is Wardenclyffe. As agents become market participants, detection shifts from human bot clusters to telling a legitimate provenance-bearing agent from a spoofed cluster of fakes, which is cluster-detection one substrate up. The per-output SHA-256 fingerprint is a stable key a model could cluster on today, and I am not aware of another coding agent that emits one under an agent loop. Its limit, because anyone serious will check: the hash includes a wall-clock timestamp taken at hash time, so a third party cannot recompute it. It is a reliable fingerprint, not yet a reproducible commitment, and provenance you cannot recompute is a label, not a price. A real hash to detect over now, an on-chain pipe to price over later that is still the open last mile.

The shorthand of one detector standing across production, verification, and settlement is the direction, not a purchase order, because two of those three layers are pre-launch or open research. The load-bearing version is smaller and truer: two sockets in shipped and referenced code, plus one honest forward bet.

## If you build the other half

If your work is behavioral detection over on-chain markets, agents, or identities, this stack is the treatment your diagnostic composes with, and the cleanest first step is the labels-and-features exchange around the open value function: research on a dataset, nothing needed on mainnet, nothing proprietary exposed to run a pilot. The public Noesis and VibeSwap code behind every reference above is there to check, and the Wardenclyffe design is in its paper. If that composition is interesting, reach out.
